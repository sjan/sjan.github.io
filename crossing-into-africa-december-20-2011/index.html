Entering Morocco through Tangiers took several hours. The processes was obviously very different from European border crossings (at least for Americans): long queues, shuffling here and there, everything happening at the whim of some border official. It reminded me of border crossings beyond Turkey on the Mongol Rally.</p>

The procedure: </p>

  1. Find your way to the front of the line (orderly or not orderly, just get there) </li>
  2. Get the attention of the border official </li>
  3. Fill out a green form. (Customs for the car) </li>
  4. Get the attention of the border official and he’ll scribble something on the form, stamp it, and throw it into the stack of green slips </li>
  5. Get directed to the police and they’ll enter you into some computer. </li>
  6. Wait a while. </li>
  7. Get the attention of the border official and maybe he’ll push your paperwork along. </li>
  8. Eventually paperwork is completed </li>
  9. Take the car over to the next line of cars </li>
  10. Exchange some money </li>
  11. Fight your way to the front of one of four car insurance booths. (requires extreme patience and tenacity) </li>
  12. Buy car insurance for 10 days. </li>
  13. done </li>
    </ol>


    </p>

    Since I had the most experience dealing with borders, I dealt with the border official. Once we got past that first phase, Kunal decided to brave the insurance line.</p>


    </p>

    The whole processes took a couple hours. It take a bit of patience but was relatively painless.</p>
